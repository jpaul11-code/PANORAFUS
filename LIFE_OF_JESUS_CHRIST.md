## Who Is Jesus Christ?

**Jesus Christ** (c. 4 BC – c. AD 30/33) is the central figure of Christianity — believed by Christians worldwide to be the Son of God, the promised Messiah of Israel, and the Savior of all humanity. His life, death, and resurrection are the foundation of the Christian faith, shaping the lives of more than **2.4 billion people** across the globe.

His name **Jesus** comes from the Hebrew *Yeshua*, meaning **"God saves."** The title **Christ** comes from the Greek *Christos*, meaning **"the Anointed One"** — equivalent to the Hebrew *Mashiach* (Messiah).

## Early Life & Upbringing

- Jesus grew up in **Nazareth**, Galilee, and was known as "Jesus of Nazareth."
- He was raised in a Jewish household, observing the Torah, attending synagogue, and keeping Jewish feasts and traditions.
- At age 12, He astonished teachers in the Temple in Jerusalem with His wisdom and understanding (Luke 2:41–52).
- He worked alongside Joseph as a **carpenter** (Mark 6:3) until His public ministry began.
- He was baptized by **John the Baptist** in the Jordan River at approximately age 30, marking the official beginning of His public ministry. The Holy Spirit descended upon Him like a dove, and the voice of God declared: *"This is my beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased."* (Matthew 3:16–17)

## The Miracles of Jesus Christ

Jesus performed **37 recorded miracles** in the Gospels, demonstrating His authority over nature, disease, death, and the spiritual realm.

### Nature Miracles
- Turned water into wine at the wedding in Cana (John 2:1–11)
- Calmed a violent storm on the Sea of Galilee (Mark 4:35–41)
- Walked on water (Matthew 14:22–33)
- Fed 5,000+ people from five loaves and two fish (John 6:1–14)
- Caused a miraculous catch of fish (Luke 5:1–11)

### Healing Miracles
- Healed ten lepers (Luke 17:11–19)
- Restored sight to the blind — including a man born blind (John 9)
- Healed a paralyzed man at the Pool of Bethesda (John 5:1–15)
- Healed a woman who had been bleeding for twelve years (Mark 5:25–34)
- Restored a withered hand (Matthew 12:9–14)
- Cleansed lepers, healed the deaf, and straightened the crippled

### Raising the Dead
- Raised **Lazarus** after four days in the tomb (John 11:1–44)
- Raised the **widow of Nain's son** (Luke 7:11–17)
- Raised the **daughter of Jairus** (Mark 5:21–43)

## The Death of Jesus Christ

Jesus was betrayed by Judas Iscariot, one of His twelve disciples, for thirty pieces of silver. He was arrested, tried before the Jewish Sanhedrin and the Roman governor Pontius Pilate, and sentenced to death — despite being found **innocent** of all charges.

| Event | Details |
|---|---|
| **The Last Supper** | Jesus shared a final Passover meal with His disciples, instituting Holy Communion (Matthew 26:26–28) |
| **Betrayal** | Judas betrayed Him with a kiss in the Garden of Gethsemane |
| **Trials** | Tried before the High Priest Caiaphas, King Herod, and Pontius Pilate |
| **Crucifixion** | Crucified at Golgotha ("The Place of the Skull") outside Jerusalem |
| **Death** | Died on the cross, bearing the sin of all humanity (c. AD 30 or 33) |
| **Burial** | Buried in the tomb of Joseph of Arimathea, sealed with a large stone |

> *"For God so loved the world that He gave His one and only Son, that whoever believes in Him shall not perish but have eternal life."* — John 3:16

### The Seven Last Words of Jesus from the Cross

1. *"Father, forgive them, for they know not what they do."* (Luke 23:34)
2. *"Today you will be with me in paradise."* (Luke 23:43)
3. *"Woman, here is your son… Here is your mother."* (John 19:26–27)
4. *"My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?"* (Matthew 27:46)
5. *"I am thirsty."* (John 19:28)
6. *"It is finished."* (John 19:30)
7. *"Father, into your hands I commit my spirit."* (Luke 23:46)

## The Resurrection of Jesus Christ

On the **third day** after His crucifixion, Jesus rose from the dead — the central miracle and cornerstone of the Christian faith.

| Event | Scripture |
|---|---|
| The empty tomb discovered by Mary Magdalene and other women | Matthew 28:1–10 |
| Jesus appears to Mary Magdalene | John 20:11–18 |
| Jesus appears to two disciples on the road to Emmaus | Luke 24:13–35 |
| Jesus appears to all eleven apostles in a locked room | John 20:19–23 |
| Jesus appears to Thomas, who had doubted | John 20:24–29 |
| Jesus appears to more than **500 people** at once | 1 Corinthians 15:6 |
| Jesus ascends to Heaven 40 days after His resurrection | Acts 1:9–11 |

> *"I am the resurrection and the life. Whoever believes in me, though he die, yet shall he live."* — John 11:25
