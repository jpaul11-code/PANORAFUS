## 1. Genealogical Roots & Family Heritage

### Tribal & Ethnic Identity

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Tribe** | Benjamin — one of the twelve tribes of Israel (Philippians 3:5) |
| **Hebrew Name** | Saul (שָׁאוּל / *Sha'ul*) — named after Israel's first king, also of the tribe of Benjamin |
| **Greek/Roman Name** | Paul (Παῦλος / *Paulos*) |
| **Ethnicity** | Hebrew of Hebrews (Philippians 3:5) — fully Jewish by blood and practice |
| **Citizenship** | Roman citizen by birth (Acts 22:28) — a rare and highly valuable status |

### Family Line (Known Relatives)

| Relation | Name / Detail | Scripture |
|---|---|---|
| **Father** | Unnamed — a Pharisee, Roman citizen, resident of Tarsus | Acts 22:3; 23:6 |
| **Mother** | Unnamed | — |
| **Sister** | Unnamed — resided in Jerusalem | Acts 23:16 |
| **Nephew** | Unnamed — warned Paul of a plot against his life | Acts 23:16–22 |
| **Kinsmen** | Andronicus, Junia, Herodion, Lucius, Jason, Sosipater | Romans 16:7, 11, 21 |

> **Note on Andronicus & Junia (Romans 16:7):** Paul calls them "my kinsmen and my fellow-prisoners, who are of note among the apostles, who also were in Christ before me." This indicates Paul had believing relatives who came to faith in Christ even before his own conversion.

### Hometown: Tarsus

- Located in Cilicia (modern-day southern Turkey)
- A prominent Greco-Roman city, renowned for its university and intellectual culture
- Paul's birth there automatically conferred Roman citizenship
- The city was a crossroads of Jewish, Greek, and Roman culture — shaping Paul's trilingual and multicultural ministry

## 2. Birth & Infancy — circa 5 AD

- **Approximate birth year:** c. 5 AD (scholars range from 5 BC to 10 AD)
- Born in **Tarsus of Cilicia** (Acts 9:11; 21:39; 22:3)
- Born into a devout Jewish family of the tribe of **Benjamin**
- His father held **Roman citizenship**, which passed to Paul at birth — an extraordinary privilege
- Circumcised on the **eighth day** according to Jewish law (Philippians 3:5)
- Given the Hebrew name **Saul** (שָׁאוּל), honoring Israel's Benjaminite king

> *"I am a Jew, born in Tarsus of Cilicia, but brought up in this city, educated at the feet of Gamaliel according to the strict manner of the law of our fathers."* — Acts 22:3

---

## 3. Childhood & Early Formation — 5–18 AD

- Raised in a **strict Pharisaic household** steeped in Torah observance
- Learned the **Hebrew Scriptures** from an early age
- Educated in the **Greek language and Hellenistic culture** through Tarsus's cosmopolitan environment
- Trained in the **tentmaking trade** (Acts 18:3) — a practical skill required of all Jewish scholars
- At some point his family relocated to or maintained strong ties with **Jerusalem**
- Likely sent to Jerusalem in early adolescence (customary for rabbinical training)

---

## 4. Education in Jerusalem — 18–30 AD

- Studied under **Gamaliel the Elder** — the most celebrated rabbi of his generation, grandson of Hillel (Acts 22:3; 5:34)
- Received **the strictest Pharisaic education** available (Acts 26:5; Philippians 3:5)
- Mastered the Torah, Oral Law (Mishnah), and prophetic writings
- Became "exceedingly zealous for the traditions of my fathers" (Galatians 1:14)
- Likely a member of the **Sanhedrin** or had close ties to it (Acts 26:10 — casting vote against believers)
- Unmarried (1 Corinthians 7:8) — though some scholars speculate he may have been widowed before his ministry

### His Credentials as a Pharisee

> *"Circumcised on the eighth day, of the people of Israel, of the tribe of Benjamin, a Hebrew of Hebrews; as to the law, a Pharisee; as to zeal, a persecutor of the church; as to righteousness under the law, blameless."* — Philippians 3:5–6

---

## 5. Pharisee & Persecutor of the Church — 30–34 AD

- Following the crucifixion and resurrection of Jesus (~30 AD) and the birth of the Church at Pentecost, Saul became a **fierce enemy of the Way** (the early Christian movement)
- **Witnessed and approved** the stoning of **Stephen** — the first Christian martyr (Acts 7:58–8:1)
- *"And Saul approved of his execution."* — Acts 8:1
- Led **house-to-house raids** in Jerusalem, dragging men and women off to prison (Acts 8:3)
- Obtained **letters of authority** from the High Priest to arrest believers in **Damascus** (Acts 9:1–2)
- Described himself later: *"I persecuted this Way to the death, binding and delivering to prison both men and women"* (Acts 22:4)

---

## 6. Damascus Road Conversion — circa 34–35 AD

One of the most pivotal events in all of human history — the dramatic encounter between Saul the persecutor and Jesus the risen Lord.

### The Event (Acts 9:1–19; 22:6–16; 26:12–18)

- Saul was traveling to Damascus with official authority to arrest Christians
- Near Damascus, a **blazing light from heaven** struck him to the ground
- He heard a voice: *"Saul, Saul, why are you persecuting me?"*
- Saul answered: *"Who are you, Lord?"*
- The voice replied: *"I am Jesus, whom you are persecuting."*
- Saul was **blinded** for three days, neither eating nor drinking
- In Damascus, a disciple named **Ananias** was sent to him
- Ananias laid hands on Saul; **scales fell from his eyes**, he received sight, was filled with the Holy Spirit, and was **baptized**
- His entire worldview, mission, and identity were transformed in an instant

> *"But whatever gain I had, I counted as loss for the sake of Christ."* — Philippians 3:7

---

## 7. Arabia & Early Discipleship — 35–38 AD

- After his conversion, Paul **did not immediately consult human authorities** (Galatians 1:16–17)
- Withdrew to **Arabia** (likely the Nabataean kingdom, near Damascus) for a period of prayer, revelation, and theological reflection
- Received the gospel *"through a revelation of Jesus Christ"* — not from human teaching (Galatians 1:12)
- Returned to Damascus and began **preaching Christ in the synagogues** (Acts 9:20)
- The Damascus Jews plotted to kill him; he **escaped over the city wall in a basket** (Acts 9:23–25; 2 Corinthians 11:32–33)

---

## 8. First Jerusalem Visit After Conversion — 38 AD

- Traveled to Jerusalem **three years after his conversion** (Galatians 1:18)
- Met with **Peter (Cephas)** for 15 days
- Also met with **James, the brother of Jesus** (Galatians 1:19)
- Attempted to join the disciples but they were afraid — until **Barnabas** vouched for him (Acts 9:26–27)
- Preached boldly in Jerusalem; Greek-speaking Jews sought to kill him
- Sent away by the brethren to **Tarsus** (Acts 9:29–30)

---

## 9. Years in Tarsus & Antioch — 38–47 AD

- Returned to his home region of **Tarsus and Cilicia** — a "silent" period of approximately 9 years
- Barnabas sought him out and brought him to **Antioch of Syria** (Acts 11:25–26)
- Together they taught for **a full year** at the church in Antioch
- **"The disciples were first called Christians in Antioch"** (Acts 11:26) — during this period
- Participated in a famine relief mission to Jerusalem with Barnabas (Acts 11:29–30) — sometimes called the "famine visit"

---

## 10. First Missionary Journey — 47–48 AD

**Commission:** The Holy Spirit set apart Paul and Barnabas for a specific mission (Acts 13:2)

| Location | Key Events |
|---|---|
| **Seleucia → Cyprus** | Departure from Antioch's port |
| **Salamis, Cyprus** | Preached in Jewish synagogues |
| **Paphos, Cyprus** | Confronted sorcerer Bar-Jesus / Elymas; proconsul Sergius Paulus believed |

| **Lystra** | Healed a lame man; mistaken for gods; Paul stoned and left for dead |

| **Return route** | Strengthened churches, appointed elders in each city |
| **Return to Antioch** | Reported all that God had done; "a door of faith to the Gentiles" |

> It is at **Paphos** that Scripture transitions from calling him "Saul" to "Paul" (Acts 13:9) — his Roman name used for Gentile mission contexts.

## 11. Jerusalem Council — 49 AD

- A critical assembly of apostles and elders in Jerusalem (Acts 15; Galatians 2)
- The question: Must Gentile believers be **circumcised and keep the Law of Moses**?
- Paul and Barnabas presented their testimony of Gentile conversions
- **James** delivered the verdict: Gentiles are **not** required to be circumcised
- A letter was sent to Gentile churches affirming freedom from the Mosaic law
- **Landmark outcome:** Established that salvation is by grace through faith alone — the theological cornerstone of Paul's ministry

## 14. Arrest in Jerusalem — 57 AD

- Paul arrived in Jerusalem with a collection from Gentile churches
- Met with James and the Jerusalem elders
- Participated in a **purification rite at the Temple** to demonstrate respect for Jewish customs
- Asian Jews recognized him, caused a riot, and **dragged him from the Temple**
- Roman commander Lysias rescued him from the mob
- Paul addressed the crowd in Hebrew — testimony of his conversion (Acts 22)
- Invoked his **Roman citizenship** to avoid scourging (Acts 22:25–29)
- Before the Sanhedrin: caused division between Pharisees and Sadducees over resurrection doctrine (Acts 23)
- **Plot to assassinate Paul** discovered by his nephew (Acts 23:12–22)
- Transferred under heavy military escort to **Caesarea Maritima**

---

## 15. Imprisonment in Caesarea — 57–59 AD

- Held in **Herod's Praetorium** in Caesarea
- Appeared before **Governor Felix** — Felix hoped for a bribe; Paul spoke of "righteousness, self-control, and the coming judgment" — Felix was alarmed (Acts 24:25)
- Felix kept Paul imprisoned for **two years**
- Appeared before new Governor **Festus** — appeal made to **Caesar** (Acts 25:11)
- Appeared before **King Agrippa II** and Bernice — Paul's famous defense (Acts 26)
- Agrippa: *"In a short time would you persuade me to be a Christian?"* (Acts 26:28)

---

## 16. Voyage to Rome & Shipwreck — 59–60 AD

- Sailed from Caesarea under Roman guard, centurion **Julius**
- Stopped at **Sidon, Myra, Fair Havens** (Crete)
- Paul warned against sailing — ignored by the centurion
- Caught in a violent storm (**Euroclydon / Northeaster**) for 14 days
- An angel appeared to Paul: *"Do not be afraid, Paul; you must stand before Caesar"* (Acts 27:24)
- **Shipwrecked on Malta** — all 276 persons survived
- On Malta: Paul **bitten by a viper** — unharmed; healed father of Publius and many others
- After three months, sailed for Rome via **Syracuse, Rhegium, Puteoli**
- Brothers from Rome met Paul at the **Forum of Appius and Three Taverns**

---

## 17. First Roman Imprisonment — 60–62 AD

- Paul lived **two full years** in his own rented house in Rome under house arrest
- Received all who came to him
- Preached the Kingdom of God and taught about Jesus "with all boldness and without hindrance" (Acts 28:31)
- **Epistles written during this imprisonment (Prison Epistles):**

| Epistle | Key Theme |
|---|---|
| **Ephesians** | The Church as the Body of Christ; spiritual warfare |
| **Philippians** | Joy in Christ; the supremacy of knowing Christ |
| **Colossians** | Christ as the fullness of God; pre-eminence of Christ |
| **Philemon** | Reconciliation; Onesimus the runaway slave |

## 18. Release & Final Journeys — 62–64 AD

Scholars believe Paul was **acquitted and released** after his first Roman imprisonment, embarking on further travels not fully recorded in Acts.

### Inferred from the Pastoral Epistles (1–2 Timothy, Titus):

| Location | Activity |
|---|---|
| **Ephesus** | Left Timothy to oversee the church; warned of false teachers (1 Timothy 1:3) |
| **Macedonia** | Traveled and wrote 1 Timothy from there |
| **Crete** | Left Titus to appoint elders in every town (Titus 1:5) |
| **Nicopolis** | Planned to winter there (Titus 3:12) |
| **Spain** | Possibly fulfilled his long-stated desire (Romans 15:24, 28) — supported by early church tradition (Clement of Rome, *1 Clement* 5:7) |
| **Troas** | Left cloak and scrolls with Carpus (2 Timothy 4:13) |
| **Corinth & Miletus** | Trophimus left sick at Miletus (2 Timothy 4:20) |

## 19. Second Roman Imprisonment & Death — 64–68 AD

### Re-Arrest

- Following the **Great Fire of Rome** (64 AD) under Emperor **Nero**, Christians were scapegoated and persecution intensified
- Paul was re-arrested — this time under far harsher conditions (2 Timothy 4:16–17)
- Held in the **Mamertine Prison** (or similar facility) in Rome

### Final Letter: 2 Timothy

Paul's last known epistle — written from prison, facing imminent death:

> *"For I am already being poured out as a drink offering, and the time of my departure has come. I have fought the good fight, I have finished the race, I have kept the faith. Henceforth there is laid up for me the crown of righteousness."* — 2 Timothy 4:6–8

### Companions at the End

| Person | Status |
|---|---|
| **Demas** | Deserted Paul (2 Timothy 4:10) |
| **Crescens** | Went to Galatia |
| **Titus** | Went to Dalmatia |
| **Luke** | Alone with Paul (2 Timothy 4:11) |
| **Mark** | Paul requested his presence |
| **Onesiphorus** | Sought Paul out, not ashamed of his chains (2 Timothy 1:16–17) |

### Martyrdom — circa 64–68 AD

- Condemned to death under Emperor **Nero**
- As a **Roman citizen**, Paul was entitled to a more dignified execution
- Tradition and early church sources (Tertullian, Eusebius, Clement of Rome) record that Paul was **beheaded** by sword outside Rome on the **Ostian Way** (Via Ostiensis)
- Feast of Saints Peter and Paul: **June 29** (Catholic/Orthodox tradition)
- Basilica of **San Paolo fuori le Mura** (St. Paul Outside the Walls) in Rome marks the traditional site of his burial

## 21. Theological Contributions

Paul's letters form the **theological backbone of Christian doctrine**. His key contributions include:

| Doctrine | Pauline Formulation |
|---|---|
| **Justification by Faith** | Righteousness apart from works of the Law (Romans 3:21–26; Galatians 2:16) |
| **Grace Alone** | Saved by grace through faith — not of works (Ephesians 2:8–9) |
| **Union with Christ** | "In Christ" — the believer's identity (Romans 6; 2 Corinthians 5:17) |
| **The Body of Christ** | The Church as one body with many members (1 Corinthians 12; Ephesians 4) |
| **Resurrection** | The foundation and hope of the Christian faith (1 Corinthians 15) |
| **The New Creation** | "If anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation" (2 Corinthians 5:17) |
| **Spiritual Gifts** | Gifts of the Spirit given for the edification of the Church (1 Corinthians 12–14) |
| **Love** | The greatest commandment and gift (1 Corinthians 13) |
| **Eschatology** | The return of Christ, the rapture, the resurrection of the dead (1 Thessalonians 4; 1 Corinthians 15) |
| **Spiritual Warfare** | The armor of God (Ephesians 6:10–18) |
